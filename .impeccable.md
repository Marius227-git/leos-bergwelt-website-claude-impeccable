# Leos Bergwelt — Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

Primarily couples or groups of close friends — 2, 4, or 6 people. The property is one floor: 3 double rooms, shared kitchen, shared bathroom, extra guest WC. Guests book 1–3 rooms together. They are looking for a real place to decompress, not a curated Instagram backdrop. They've probably been burned by generic Airbnbs that looked different in person. They want to know they're booking somewhere with actual character and a real person behind it.

**Context of use**: Discovery and booking happen on desktop or phone, often in the evening after a stressful week. The guest is already daydreaming about the mountains before they land on the page.

**Job to be done**: Convince them — emotionally, not rationally — that this place will give them exactly what they need: quiet, fresh air, and the feeling of being somewhere that doesn't try too hard.

### Brand Personality

**Three words**: *unhurried · honest · rooted*

Like a friend who lives in the mountains — calm, grounded, and never trying too hard. The brand is a person, not a company. It's Leo (the actual host). It's a farmhouse floor in Tiefenbach that's been cared for, not designed by committee.

**Emotional goal**: The moment a visitor lands on the page, their shoulders should drop. Not excitement — *relief*. "This is the place."

### Aesthetic Direction

**Tone**: Editorial alpine — the visual language of a well-made hiking guidebook or a small Austrian inn that's been written about in a travel magazine once and doesn't advertise. Warm, unhurried, slightly worn in the right places.

**Theme**: Light. The mountains are best experienced in morning light, not in a dark spa brochure. The palette should feel like early-morning fog clearing over the valley — warm stone, deep forest, a single amber accent.

**Reference (liked)**: luxestay.framer.website — generous whitespace, numbered editorial sections, evocative sparse language, photography-forward with restrained UI chrome.

**Anti-references**:
- Generic Airbnb listing feel (bullet points, star ratings, platform UI patterns)
- Crowded booking platform aesthetic (cramped, price-forward, urgency tricks)
- Polished-but-soulless hotel brand (sanitized, corporate, over-designed)

**What makes it unforgettable**: The page feels like the place itself — quiet, grounded, with just enough personality to make you trust the person who built it.

### Design Principles

1. **Stillness over spectacle.** No aggressive CTAs, no urgency copy, no flashy transitions. The page doesn't sell — it lets you arrive.

2. **The landscape is the product.** Photography leads. Typography and layout serve the images, never compete with them. White space is not empty — it's breathing room.

3. **Friends, not guests.** The voice is direct and warm, like a recommendation from someone you trust. Copy is short and honest. Nothing reads like marketing.

4. **One real accent, used sparingly.** A single amber/ember tone — the color of evening light on mountain stone — is the only chromatic statement. Everything else is neutral.

5. **Earned complexity.** Interactions and animations should feel inevitable, not decorative. A scroll-driven rotation, a slow crossfade, a nav that earns its blur — nothing that calls attention to itself.

### Technical Context

- **Stack**: Plain HTML/CSS/JS (no framework). Target migration to Next.js later.
- **Fonts in use** (to replace when refreshing): Newsreader, Figtree, JetBrains Mono
- **Served via**: `npx serve` on port 3000 (`.claude/launch.json` configured)
- **Assets**: All images in `assets/images/`, logo in `assets/images/logo/`
